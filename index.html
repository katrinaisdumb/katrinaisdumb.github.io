About

My name is Ian Feekes, I am a third year computer science student at University of California Santa Cruz. I love to program and to learn new things, and I always find it exciting to explore new coding languages and techniques. I originally learned Java in high scool, and in college I branched out to working on projects in C and C++ in both a linux-based environment, and using Xcode.

Outside of school I spend a lot of time coding as part of personal projects, both out of interest and to sharpen my skills for practical use. I was introduced to javascript for web applications as may be seen in this website. I am very interested in gaming, and as a result spend a lot of time learning C++ and Unreal Engine 4 conventions for projects that I am currently developing.

As part of my effort to perfect my skills as well as possible, I have worked on several online courses, including codecademy tutorials that I have posted below in the website. They have proven especially useful in learning both web development skills and tricks for manipulating files in a command-line environment.

Projects

  • Unreal Engine 4

    • EX One of my biggest interests is in game design. I love developing video games, and ever since I took some introductory classes in University, I started dissecting games and taking apart their mechanics whenever I got the chance. I downloaded the widely popular Unreal Engine 4 game development software and began writing code for my own projects in C++.

    I am currently working on creating my own resource-based game called "Feywild," inspired from the likes of Settlers of Catan that has mechanics like strategic placement of cities near resource centers and the production of armies to blockade your opponent's resources. I combine this with polishing the graphics to create aesthetic, randomly-generated wilderness tiles containing seeds for different terrains (which yield various resources) intended to make gameplay competitive, exciting and attractive.

    An idea I am working towards with this project is using AI opponents that are challenging to defeat - without making the game inherently unfair - to produce more of a pleasure of overcoming challenge for the player. I want to implement this in a unique way so that the CNN does not play predictably as is seen in so many games. To make the bots smart I intend on creating a "neural network" learning system to give the outcome of intelligent, challenging gameplay that constatly engages the players and evolves with their skill levels.
