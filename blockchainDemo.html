Block Chains

Block Chains are a new, flashy way to store data. This technology was initially invented for bitcoin transactions, however it is useful in many other areas. It allows you to store blocks of related data (bitcoin transactions, private profile data, etc.) in a unique way

Distributed

Block chain is unique in that it is distributed. You can essentially understand this definition as "it can exist as something that is present on everyone's devices." There is no one specific server/computer that serves as a higher authority. This is convenient because to most extend, transactions on block chain are publically recorded. Rather than purchasing a big flashy super computer to do the heavy lifting, like many banks and government departments do, transactions can be verified with anyone's computer.

This means that your computer can potentially work to verify a transaction made by someone else around the world. Typically, for this work, or "mining", that your computer does, you will be rewarded with cryptocurrency. This is part of the reason why graphics cards became so expensive when cryptocurrency was becoming very popular. People would use graphics cards to allow their computer to do mining around the clock, and could essentially make money with only having to invest in graphics cards.

Secure

Because of the fancy structure and math behind the way blockchains work they are very secure. Obviously with things like monetary transactions, some people might be interested in altering these digital records and making some money - especially if the system is not regulated by a single central entity. Through linking each block (transaction) through a hash function, it is very very difficult to tamper these records. If they are tampered with, due to their structure, it is also very easy to isolate the exact piece of data where any tampering may have occured.
